Key Data Requirements for Calculating Footprint Entry

The key data requirements for calculating footprint entry include...

Energy Consumption

According to the U.S Energy Infromation Administration, in 2020 alone, the U.S power sector emitted 1,448 million metric tons of carbon dioxide. This accounts for about 32% of total U.S
energy-related CO₂ emmisions. Power plants, burned coal, natural gas, and petroleum fuels are the source of 62% of total U.S electricity generation, but accounts for 99% of U.S electricity
related CO₂. About 0.92 pounds of CO₂ is generatedper kWh.

Transportation

A typical U.S passenger vehicle emits about 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide per year. This number can vary based off a vehicle’s driving distance, fuel economy, and type of fuel. One gallon
of gasoline produces 8,887 grams of CO₂, and one gallon of diesel produces 10,180 grams CO₂. Roughly 404 grams of CO₂ is emitted through the average passenger vehicle.

Production Emissions

According to the U.S Energy Information Administration, the burning of fossil fuels and production of heat causes severe environmental effects. Coal emits around 216lbs of CO₂ /Btu, diesel
emits around 161.3lbs of CO₂/Btu, gasoline produces around 157.2lbs of CO₂/Btu, propane produces around 139lbs of CO₂/Btu, and natural gas produces around 117lbs of CO₂/Btu.
Although these emission are produced mainly from electrical producers, other manfucaturing facilities may contribute.
