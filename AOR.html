Terra's Action Oriented Report

Our plan consists of 4 main steps, with 2 extra ones.

1. Locating Country of Origin

Certain factors that affect environmental footprints - such as electricity and gas consumption - vary between countries. This is due to the fact that each country has their own methods of generating and consuming
these resources. Thus, we began our approach in calculating an organizations environmental footprint by first locating their country of origin.

2. Coming up With a Formula

Utilizing the information from this resource and this one, we incorporated a function into our code that takes the amount of CO₂ wasted via electricity, gas, and gasoline consumption to calculate how much CO₂
is produced per kWH. This is where the country of origin comes into play - our code contains a function that allows a certain formula to be executed in accordance to what country a business is from in order to
obtain a more accurate answer.

3. Calculating CO₂ Production Over Time

Our code will then execute additional functions that will predict how much will be CO₂ produced over the course of a week, a year, and five years. We implemented these features to allow organizations to not only
focus on current actions to deal with CO₂ consumption, but future actions to take in the long run as well.

4. Profitability

The cost of our services are completely free. To keep the site running, we will rely on donations. The way in which we gather data for our calculations makes our environmental footprint calculator very cheap to run. In
turn, this will encourage companies from all over to use our calculator, which promotes good health for our Earth. Overall, we at Terra support the businesses' that initiative to save our planet, which is why we are happy
to make this resource available to all organizations.

5. Additional Details

To increase flexibility and convenience for users, our code allows commas to be contained within values for easier viewing. Furthermore, our code utilizes clear, descriptive language, so that any one, even non-expert
users in the area of environmental footprints, can use our application, while also understanding thoroughly and in a simple manner.
In regards to validation testing to identify incorrectly entered data, we have implemented a notification box relaying to users the values they've inputted to ensure they're satisfied, or if they've inputted the wrong
information or nothing at all, they will be alerted. In the event of the latter cases, the user's previous values will be deleted and a warning will pop up.

6. Future Features

To further refine our code and increase accuracy in our results, in the future some of the things we will implement will include allowing for the input of more values of factors that affect environmental footprints, for
example, how waste production coincides with the production of CO₂.
To improve understandability among users, we plan to add visuals, such as a wide range of graphs when displaying results, and this will be applicable not just for the present moment, but also to display an interval over
a period of time.
Terra is still in it's early stages. As time passes, Terra will be able to consider many countries within it's calculator from all across the globe.
For additional opportunities for profit, Terra is planning to partner with other environment-based organizations for sponsorships, as well as utilizing ad revenue on our site.
